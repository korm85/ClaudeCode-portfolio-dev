Frame every outcome around the specific decision Michael made, not what the product does. "98% reduction in review time" must be followed by "achieved by [the filtering architecture Michael specified]" — not left as a bare stat.

**Why:** The site reads as a product brochure when copy describes features. A PM audience evaluates decisions and their outcomes, not product capabilities.

**How to apply:**
- Metric → attribution clause: who made the call and what was the mechanism
- Key decision bullets → explain the trade-off, not just the choice
- Avoid language that could describe any PM's product ("designed the system", "built the roadmap") — describe what only Michael would have done here

No em dashes anywhere in copy. Use colons, semicolons, or commas.

Target audience is general PM/hiring-manager, not 3D printing domain experts. Soften domain jargon: "additive manufacturing" → "manufacturing", "thermo-mechanical solver" → "simulation module that predicts heat and distortion simultaneously".
